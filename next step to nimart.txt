The Roadmap to a Branded Login
This process requires you to provide some key information to Google for their review. It typically takes 2-3 business days.

Prerequisites: Get Your Docs Ready: Before you start, ensure you have accessible URLs for:

Homepage: https://nimart.ng

Privacy Policy: https://nimart.ng/privacy (Create this page if it doesn't exist)

Terms of Service: https://nimart.ng/terms (Create this page if it doesn't exist)

App Logo: A square image (ideally 120x120px) of the Nimart logo.

Navigate to the Branding Section: Go to the Google Cloud Console. Make sure the correct project is selected, then navigate to "APIs & Services" → "OAuth consent screen". Once there, look for the "Branding" option in the left sidebar.

Configure Brand Information: In the Branding section, fill out the following:

App Name: Enter "Nimart".

User support email: Select a support email address for users to contact.

Upload Logo: Upload the Nimart logo you prepared.

Homepage URL: Enter https://nimart.ng.

Privacy Policy URL: Enter the URL for your privacy policy.

Terms of Service URL: Enter the URL for your terms of service (this is optional but recommended).

Authorized Domains: Add nimart.ng to this list. This is a critical step, as Google will need to verify your ownership of this domain.

Verify Domain Ownership (Crucial Step): You must prove to Google that you own nimart.ng. This is done through the Google Search Console.

Go to the Google Search Console.

Add nimart.ng as a new property.

Follow the instructions to verify ownership (the most common method is adding a TXT record to your DNS settings). Once verified, this will link your domain to your Google Cloud project.

Submit for Verification: Once all the information is filled out and your domain is verified, scroll down and click "Submit for verification". Google's Trust and Safety team will review your application.

✨ Important Considerations & Potential Issues
Publishing Status: Ensure your app's publishing status is set to "In Production". If it's in "Testing," only test users will see the branded screen.

Homepage URL Rejection: A common issue is Google rejecting the homepage URL as "unresponsive." If this happens, double-check that your nimart.ng homepage loads quickly, is accessible globally, and clearly describes what Nimart is. You can then resubmit after updating the URL in the Cloud Console.

Pending Developer Action: If you get stuck in this state, it usually means Google is waiting for more information from you. Check the email inbox associated with your Google Cloud project for a message from api-oauth-dev-verification@google.com.

Patience is Key: The review process takes time, and initial rejections are common. Just address any feedback from Google and resubmit.

Once this process is complete, your users will see "Nimart" on the OAuth consent screen, which is a huge win for professionalism and user trust. Good luck with the submission, and let me know if you hit any snags.
